**The uncountable:**

2. **The 2am debugging sessions:** When someone finally understood the gamma function because of a post—unmeasured.
3. **The deleted comment section:** Years of conversation, removed—gone.
4. **The posts that mattered most:** Probably not the longest or most-tagged.
5. **The influence on students:** Graduate students who cite these posts in their heads, not their papers.

**Supporting data (what the numbers DO show about the edges):**

**Shortest posts (the other extreme):**
- 5 words: "Music recommendations"
- 7 words: "Synchronizing metronomes"

232 posts under 50 words—announcements, links, pointers. The data captures these, but not their impact.

**Tags used exactly once (79 total):**
Sage, Leadership, CSS, Decision theory, Neuroplasticity, Debugging, Etymology, Coq, Java...

Each one represents a curiosity that didn't recur. The long tail of a mind.

**Series posts (66 total):**
"Part II," "Part III," "continued"—threads the author wanted to extend. But which threads mattered most to readers?

**Narrative beats:**
1. **The limits of quantification:** This book shows patterns, not meaning
2. **The invisible influence:** Infrastructure without metrics
3. **The removed comment section:** A deliberate choice—what was lost?
4. **The territory vs. the map:** We've drawn a map. The territory is larger.
5. **Why this book exists anyway:** Patterns suggest stories. Stories suggest a person. That's enough.
